Why does sample size matter?

  • Standard error shrinks as n grows: SE(β̂) ∝ 1/√n. More data → tighter estimates.
  • Confidence interval narrows: the 95% CI is β̂ ± t* × SE(β̂). Watch it shrink as you increase n.
  • t-statistic grows: t = β̂ / SE(β̂). The same slope becomes more detectable with larger n.
  • P-value falls as the t-statistic moves further into the tail of the t-distribution.
  • With a tiny sample the true relationship may appear non-significant by chance (Type II error).
  • With a huge sample even a trivially small coefficient will be statistically significant—always report effect sizes!
