abstract: This study uses a conjoint experimental design to disentangle the relative influence of recommender attributes (e.g., peer recommendations, algorithmic recommendations) and news-story attributes (e.g., topic, outlet) on users' decisions to read and share news on social networking sites. The results show that both recommender and news-story attributes matter, with peer recommendations having particularly strong effects on sharing decisions. The study contributes to our understanding of how social and algorithmic curation jointly shape news exposure.
