abstract: This study examines how the rally-round-the-flag phenomenon — the temporary surge in public support for authorities during national crises — shapes trust in the news media. Using panel survey data collected before and during the COVID-19 pandemic in Norway, we show that the crisis was associated with increased trust in the news media, and that this effect was strongest among citizens who were initially less trusting. The findings illuminate how national crises can reshape attitudes towards journalistic institutions.
