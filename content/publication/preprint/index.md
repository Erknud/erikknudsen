abstract: This article investigates how news recommender systems shape selective exposure to political news. Drawing on two online experiments, it models the conditional effects of recommendation-based curation on users' news consumption. Results suggest that news recommenders moderate the relationship between political interest and news exposure, potentially amplifying selective exposure among the most politically engaged audiences. The findings have important implications for understanding the democratic role of algorithmic news curation.
