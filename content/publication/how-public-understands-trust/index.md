abstract: While there is considerable interest in public trust in the news media, little is known about what citizens actually mean when they say they trust or distrust news media. Using open-ended survey responses from a large Norwegian sample, this article inductively explores how the public understands news media trust. The analysis reveals a rich landscape of trust-relevant considerations, including accuracy and impartiality, journalistic independence, and the media's role in democracy.
